About Me

My Journey

I am a former healthcare therapist and manager turned User Experience designer, finding all the creativity in life. My outlets for creativity in healthcare management came with innovating new folding techniques for towels and sheets so seconds were saved when using them with patients, so efficiency is a large part of my design outlook.

I have a BS in Kinesiology and have worked in the healthcare field, physical therapy in particular, for the past decade. Educating patients on their body movements and the most efficient ways to move and recover nicely dove tails into UX design, creating efficient navigation and design while interviewing and really listening to what clients want and their purpose for recovery or design.

While a manager, I oversaw 27 employees ranging from Human Resources and payroll, to accounting, scheduling and inventory management sprinkled with a healthy dose of maintenance. Having worn so many different hats, I am prepared to step into any role that may come my way.

Lacking a real creative outlook in my work began to take a toll though and I found myself browsing more remote options to foster my creative talents. After finding the CareerFoundry UX Immersion course, I knew this would be something I could sink my teeth into and really get a start on a design course to change my career path. Though it was a decade in the healthcare industry, that time taught me about empathy, management, time-efficiency, Human Resources and a plethora of other skills that are challenging to gather throughout a career and life.
